# Fleet Captain — Context Management & Self-Evolution

_Research synthesis · 2026-06-08 · multi-agent workflow (13 agents, ~694k tokens, 6 facets → adversarial verify → synthesize). Nous/Hermes + Karpathy + SOTA._

> Verifier flagged as **fabricated/refuted** and NOT to build on: a universal "200K window degrades near 50K" threshold (use empirical, per-Captain occupancy instead) and "instant/background compaction" (compaction is a real summarization step with drift risk — prefer pointer-to-disk).

## Headline
Nous ships **Hermes Agent** (open-source, MIT, `github.com/NousResearch/hermes-agent`, v0.16.0 dated 2026-06-05) — a self-evolving agent whose architecture is almost exactly the Captain we've been converging on. It validates the direction and gives a concrete, studyable reference.

## Track 1 — Context management
### Key findings
- Fleet ALREADY has the right two-tier memory split in principle: project-specific durable memory lives in per-project CLAUDE.md + .claude-docs/ (loaded by workers, NOT resident in the Captain), and project-AGNOSTIC doctrine lives in skills/fleet/orchestrator-doctrine.md. The context-residue problem is therefore not a missing architecture — it's that nothing MECHANICALLY prevents project residue (worker transcripts, file dumps, verify logs) from accumulating in the Captain's live window. The doctrine relies on exhortation ('stay in orchestrator mode'), not enforcement. This is exactly the gap the verified Anthropic/Letta/Context-Folding findings address.
- The daemon's waveCompleteMessage (src/daemon/policy.ts:28) is the single most important existing surface for BOTH tracks. It already fires once per wave, is token-free, and already nudges a project-memory distill. It is the natural hook to (a) inject ONLY a compact wave digest into the Captain instead of raw transcripts, and (b) trigger periodic self-evolution reflection. Both tracks should attach here rather than inventing new loops.
- fleet capture (src/commands/capture.ts) is currently a ONE-SHOT stub: it scaffolds a SKILL.md with a TODO and a verbatim copy of the worker's task — no eval gate, no utility tracking, no decay. This directly matches the verified 'library drift' risk (LLM-authored skills can net to +0.0pp and degrade retrieval, vs +16.2pp for curated). Capture needs the MUSE 4-stage lifecycle (create→memory→manage→evaluate) and a Voyager-style independent verification gate before a skill is promoted — Fleet already has the verifier primitive (fleet verify / judge≠generator doctrine) to build it.
- The registry (src/registry.ts) tracks per-agent task/status/timestamps but stores NO outcome, NO verify verdict, and NO token cost. There is no durable record of 'this delegation succeeded/failed and why.' Self-evolution needs an append-only delegation/outcome log (the trajectory store the Atropos/DGM findings point at) — it does not exist yet and is the foundational missing piece for any evidence-gated doctrine improvement.
- Fleet's gates already 'fail closed' (audit-docs, currency exit non-zero on inconclusive) and the doctrine already enforces judge≠generator and staged verification. This means the SAFEST verified self-evolution patterns (DGM staged gate, Voyager separate-critic, MUSE evaluation stage) map onto primitives Fleet already has — the work is wiring them into capture/doctrine edits, not building verification from scratch.
- Two embellished findings must NOT be built on: the '200K window degrades near 50K' threshold is fabricated (Chroma explicitly declines a universal number) — any context-eviction policy must be EMPIRICAL/configurable against the Captain's own occupancy, never a hard-coded token count; and the 'instant/background compaction' claim is unsupported — compaction is a real summarization step with drift risk, so prefer pointer-to-disk over re-summarization.
- The Captain has no searchable index over its own history. The single best-grounded Hermes finding (FTS5 session search + LLM summarization, confirmed verbatim in the repo) maps to a missing Fleet capability: a queryable index over past waves/objectives/captured skills so the Captain can ask 'have I orchestrated this before?' and pull a compact playbook instead of holding history resident.

## Track 2 — Self-evolution
### Key findings
- The safest-to-least-safe ordering of self-evolution is empirically grounded and maps cleanly onto Fleet: ADDITIVE skill capture (Voyager) is safest, EVIDENCE-GATED doctrine deltas (DGM archive + staged gate) are next, and FREE-FORM self-rewriting of live operating instructions (Gödel Agent) is the verified anti-pattern to AVOID (the paper itself reports instability and error accumulation). Fleet should self-evolve only through additive, gated, reversible mechanisms — never by rewriting orchestrator-doctrine.md in place without a gate.
- The single most-cited, cross-verified principle across five independent facets is: NEVER let the proposer be the sole judge. Voyager's separate-GPT-4 critic (ablating it drops results ~73%), Meta-Rewarding (self-judge saturates; 22.9→39.4 only with a meta-judge), and DGM's staged gate all confirm it. Fleet already encodes judge≠generator in doctrine and has fleet verify — so the self-evolution gate is a wiring job, not a research risk.
- fleet capture today promotes on a single success with no held-out re-test — exactly the 'library drift' failure (verified: LLM-authored skills +0.0pp vs human-curated +16.2pp, single-paper but directionally consistent). The fix is the verified MUSE 4-stage lifecycle (create→memory→manage→evaluate): a captured skill must pass an independent re-run on a held-out task vs a no-skill baseline before it becomes resident/retrievable.
- Karpathy's autoresearch loop gives the exact template for evidence-gated doctrine evolution, verified verbatim from program.md: edit ONE narrow surface (train.py), fixed time budget, score a single metric (val_bpb), keep-or-git-reset. The Fleet analog: a doctrine-delta agent edits ONLY a doctrine deltas file (not project facts), is scored by fleet verify against held-out past objectives, and merges only if a fleet metric (wave success rate / tokens-per-objective / escalation count) improves.
- There is NO trajectory/outcome store today (registry holds no verdict or cost). Every verified self-improvement mechanism — DGM archive+lineage, Atropos trajectory→reward, MUSE evaluation, the metacognitive failure-taxonomy (Liu & van der Schaar 2506.05109, byline corrected) — REQUIRES a durable record of what was delegated, what happened, and the verify verdict. This append-only log is the prerequisite for the entire track; without it, self-evolution has no evidence to gate on.
- Skills must DECAY, not just accrete. Memp/ReMe verified utility-based scoring + pruning; Library Drift motivates retirement of net-negative skills. Fleet has fleet audit-docs/currency as the periodic-gate pattern already — extend that pattern to a skill-currency audit that scores each captured skill's realized reuse success and quarantines stale/negative ones, giving the library garbage collection instead of unbounded growth.
- Several precise numeric thresholds in the findings are explicitly flagged unverified (Hermes '5+ tool calls', '~every 15 tasks'; the '50K' context number; '~8% Reflexion boost' as a global stat). Self-evolution triggers and cadences must therefore be TUNED against Fleet's own logs, not copied — the behaviors (nudge-to-capture, periodic self-audit) are real, the magic numbers are not.

## Open questions (decide before building)
- What concrete fleet metric should gate doctrine deltas — wave success rate, tokens-per-objective, escalation count, or a composite? The verified autoresearch/DGM pattern needs ONE scored metric; choosing the wrong one invites metric-hacking. Needs a small held-out objective suite to even measure.
- How is a 'held-out task instance' for the capture eval gate and doctrine gate actually obtained? Voyager/MUSE assume a re-runnable benchmark; many Fleet delegations are one-of-a-kind project tasks with no second instance to test against. Without re-runnable evals, the gate degrades to 'did the same task pass twice,' which the findings explicitly warn against.
- Where do Captain memory-block caps and the compaction trigger get their thresholds? The findings explicitly fabricate the '50K' number and flag Hermes '5+ calls'/'~15 tasks' as unverified — so these must be tuned against the Captain's own occupancy/logs, but no such telemetry is collected yet. What minimal telemetry is needed first?
- Is a separate standing Auditor session worth its always-on token cost for a single-user Max-plan fleet, or should auditing be a periodic batch job over the outcome log? Meta-Rewarding justifies the separate evaluator, but Fleet's hibernation economics favor token-free batch.
- Does FTS5 (a SQLite extension) fit Fleet's 'zero runtime deps, pure-ESM, no build' constraint, or does the searchable-history index need a dependency-free approach (e.g., a JSON-lines + grep/ripgrep index)? The CLAUDE.md zero-dep rule may conflict with the strongest-grounded Hermes pattern.
- How are doctrine deltas reconciled when the Captain works across many projects with different conventions — does a delta proven on project A's objectives risk overfitting doctrine to A? The project-agnostic scoping rule helps but the held-out suite composition determines whether 'general' lessons are actually general.

_Fleet ideas:_
- Adopt the Voyager promotion gate for `fleet capture`: a worker/workflow is promoted to a reusable skill ONLY after an independent verifier (a separate cheap Claude session running `fleet verify`, NOT the worker that produced it) confirms success against the original objective. This mirrors Voyager's generate->self-verify->only-then-add-to-library invariant (verified: separate GPT-4 critic; ablating it drops results ~73%) and prevents capturing flaky one-offs.
- Give every captured skill a utility score and decay/prune policy (from Memp/ReMe utility-based refinement): track invocation count, reuse success rate, and last-used date in the per-session registry. Auto-demote or archive skills whose realized reuse success drops below threshold. This is the exact prune-on-realized-usefulness step ReMe (2512.10696) adds on top of append-only memory, stopping the library from bloating with skills that looked good once but don't generalize.
- Keep a DGM-style ARCHIVE of Captain doctrine versions, not just the latest. Every self-evolution edit becomes an immutable archive entry with full lineage (what changed, why, which objective triggered it). Selection for which doctrine to run can branch from older stepping-stone versions, and any regression is a one-line rollback to a prior archive node. This gives DGM's verified 'traceable lineage of modifications for review' as the core safety mechanism.
- Stage doctrine self-edits exactly like DGM stages child agents: a proposed change first runs a tiny smoke set (does the Captain still spawn/grid/watch correctly?), then a medium held-out set of real objectives, and only edits that improve the held-out suite without regressing the smoke set get adopted. Never adopt a self-edit validated only on the same objective that motivated it — that is the benchmark/objective hacking DGM and Gödel explicitly warn about (verified caveat).
- Split 'doer' from 'judge' in the fleet, applying the verified Meta-Rewarding lesson (self-judge alone saturates): the Captain proposes improvements, but a separate long-lived 'Auditor' session evaluates whether captured skills and doctrine edits actually helped, and periodically the Captain critiques the Auditor's judgments to keep evaluation quality from saturating.
- Fix context residue with a Reflexion-style externalization boundary: the Captain's working context stays lean and project-agnostic; after each wave it writes a short verbal reflection ('what worked / what to do differently') NOT into its own context but into the project's durable memory (CLAUDE.md / .claude-docs/). Project-specific learning lives in per-project durable memory; only project-AGNOSTIC management lessons flow back into Captain doctrine — the episodic-vs-procedural split the foundation-agent memory survey (2602.06052) describes.
- Add a metacognitive loop to the token-free daemon (from Liu & van der Schaar 2506.05109): on each wave-complete/escalation event, run a structured monitor step (which workers failed and why, categorized) and a bounded-confidence check (did I actually verify this, or am I assuming?). Failures get categorized into a durable failure taxonomy that informs future delegation — turning the heartbeat into a monitoring/control cycle rather than just a nudge engine.
- Make `fleet bootstrap` seed new projects from the captured-skill archive ADAS-style: retrieve the top-k most relevant project-agnostic skills/workflows from the archive into the new project's context as stepping stones, instead of cold-starting. The archive of proven workflows becomes the Captain's transferable design library, analogous to ADAS's archive that seeds new agent designs (verified: +13.6 F1 / +14.4% from archive-seeded search, with cross-domain transfer).
- Hard-gate the Captain's self-evolution to project-AGNOSTIC artifacts only: doctrine, skills, and CLI/tool patterns may self-evolve; project-specific facts may NOT enter Captain doctrine. Enforce with a scoped-edit constraint mirroring DGM's verified 'self-improvement limited to modifying the coding codebase' — a narrow, declared edit surface is the single biggest practical safety lever.
- Treat unconstrained Gödel-Agent-style self-rewriting as the anti-pattern to avoid in production: the paper itself reports it is unstable and prone to error accumulation (verified self-stated limitation). The Captain should self-evolve only through the archive+verify+stage+rollback discipline above (additive, gated, reversible), never by free-form rewriting of its own live operating instructions in place.
