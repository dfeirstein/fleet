L.I.F.T. — audit any composition against four letters

Run a finished layout through four questions. Each must pass; a single failing letter is where the composition leaks attention. The amber letter is the prompt, the question is the test, and the last line is the tell that you failed it.

L
Leverage pointOne thing wins
Does ONE element clearly win — by scale, contrast, or isolation?
Fails iftwo elements tie. Then there is no leverage point — dim, shrink, or space the competitor away until one wins.
I
Internal rhythmBeat + accent
Does steady alignment set a calm beat, with one deliberate break as the accent?
Fails ifnothing repeats (no rhythm) or nothing breaks (monotony). Repetition sets the beat; one break is the emphasis.
F
Friction & flowGrab, then breathe
One intense focal point to grab, and whitespace to breathe — both, in sequence?
Fails ifit is all friction (fatigue) or all flow (the eye drifts). A layout needs both, sequenced — impact, then pause.
T
TransferabilitySurvives anywhere
Does it survive shrinking, dark / light bg, and mobile / print / motion?
Fails ifit only works at one size on one background. A composition that breaks off its design size is not done.
