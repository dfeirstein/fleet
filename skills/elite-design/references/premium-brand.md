# Premium / luxury / flagship brand

The ~10 rules that matter most when the goal is a premium, luxury, or flagship
brand feel, with numbers. Load this for high-end brand sites where the emotional
payload outranks raw conversion. **This is a floor — the brand brief sets direction
and wins any conflict.**

## Core context rules
- **Manufacture premium via cognitive fluency:** maximize ease-of-processing — the
  lazy brain reads ease as quality and effort-to-parse as cheap/untrustworthy. So
  the craft is extreme whitespace, one accent, one CTA, two-tier contrast. **MORE
  information DECREASES trust; subtraction is the craft** — adding badges/copy to
  "prove value" lowers perceived quality.
- **Land the verdict in the 50ms–2s window (Halo Effect)** and never break it.
  Engineer for the **Peak-End Rule** — plant small peaks of delight
  (micro-interactions: green-checkmark inline form confirmation with a soft-green
  border, hover elevation, directional scroll reveals) and obsessively nail the
  final step.
- **Extreme whitespace as an assertion of dominance** (Hermès / Bottega 'we don't
  need to shout') — massive margins, products/words float. Section vertical padding
  **~120px+ (py-24/py-32)**, card/grid gutters **gap-8–gap-16 (~32–64px)**. The
  deliberate opposite of the conversion instinct to fill every pixel.
- **Near-monochrome canvas** where the single saturated element is the CTA
  out-classes any colorful branded page; secondary surfaces = a tint of the ONE
  brand color.
- **Genuine/messy real photography beats polished stock or AI studio shots** — a
  slightly imperfect real photo (messy desk, dimly-lit real team) proves real
  humans work there and converts better. **No graphics > bad graphics** —
  congruency rule: delete any graphic that clashes with brand tone.
- **Brand-aura mode** (when the goal is flagship, NOT raw conversion):
  de-emphasized CTAs, cryptic pure-black editorial layouts, a fixed **bottom pill
  nav** (bottom:20px, backdrop-blur, border-radius:50px), a floating focal element
  in a black void that fades + scales-down + locks on scroll (Stryds / Apple).
- **Inverse line-height scale** (tighter as bigger): **H1 102pt/104% Bold, H2
  64pt/106% SemiBold, H3 40pt/110% Medium, H4 26.6pt/120% Medium, body 16px/160%
  Regular** — never one uniform leading ratio across sizes. Negative tracking on
  display. One geometric-sans display (e.g. Hurme Geometric Sans 3) + a neutral
  legible body sans (Inter) ONLY with rationale; headlines/subheads paired with
  Inter no smaller than 26pt SemiBold.
- **In 2026, stripping motion IS premium:** limit to subtle scroll fade-ins,
  skeleton/sequential loading, and crisp hover (color/opacity shift or gentle
  elevation). Ban spinning, bouncing, scroll-jacking, heavy parallax, autoplay 3D
  (Spline/Rive). Spectacle motion only if engineered, damped, and tied to genuine
  3D storytelling.
- **White itself can be the 10% ACCENT** on a colored/dark section (Lemon Squeezy:
  60% purple, 30% yellow, 10% white) — not just a background.

## Color by function, contrast by math
- **Assign every color a FUNCTIONAL role before picking a hue** — Primary,
  Secondary, Highlight, Soft Neutral, Divider, Text Body — e.g. #1E2A3A, #4F6C83,
  #D9B78B, #F5F6F8, #E0E4E8, #2A3340. Restrain to **2–3 muted structural colors**
  (black/white/off-white/dark slate) + EXACTLY ONE deliberate accent reserved for
  highlights + CTA buttons. Never 5 saturated hues at once.
- **Two-tier text contrast** as an automatic eye-guide: near-black headlines
  (gray-900) + mid-gray descriptive copy (gray-500/600).
- **Verify contrast mathematically against WCAG, never by eye:** **7:1** for
  body/small text, **4.5:1** for large text/headers.

## Hero + copy discipline (premium framing)
- **Hero = exactly 4 pieces:** one dominant headline, a subheadline, one
  full-screen image/video, one CTA — nothing else competing above the fold. Cap
  headline at **1–3 words for luxury** (5–8 for tech/SaaS); H1 in **text-6xl–7xl**
  (~3–4× body). Answer 'What is this? Who is it for? Why care?' within the
  50ms–3s window.
- **One primary CTA**; pre-set the next step; demote secondaries to ghost/text.
- **Write copy bold enough to REPEL the wrong client** — name one specific avatar
  and aggressively tell unqualified people they're not a fit. Repelling is a
  feature, not a bug.
- **Cut homepage copy ~75%**; punchy headlines, short sentences, bolded keywords,
  written for scanners.
- **Show pricing / a minimum** to filter leads ('Investment: $5,000/month
  minimum'); never gate price behind 'Book a Call'.

## Process
- **Mobile-first as a psychological content filter** (assume >60% / ~62% mobile),
  not just a technical requirement — its value is forcing ruthless elimination of
  clutter before scaling up. Test on 2 bars of 4G; keep load **<3s** (every 1s of
  delay costs ~7% of conversions, >3s loses ~half of visitors).
- **Wireframe in gray boxes** (no color, no real copy) on a strict 12-col grid to
  answer what the eye lands on 1st/2nd/3rd; build a clickable prototype before
  production code.
- A website is **not a brochure or a layout — it's an engineered emotional STATE**
  (calm / confidence / trust). Clarity itself is the design, even when it costs the
  award-winning look.

## The divergence to decide (by goal)
**Brand-aura vs conversion:** this context leans **aura** — de-emphasized CTAs,
editorial pure-black, whitespace-as-luxury — when the goal is a flagship brand.
If the same page is actually a paid-traffic funnel, flip to the conversion side
(one clear CTA, transparent pricing) per `references/landing.md`. Pick by goal;
don't flatten.
